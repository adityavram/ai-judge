# WUDC/BP Speaker Scale Reference

## Overview
- Speaker scores range from 50 to 100, average is 75
- Whole numbers only
- Scores below 50 or above 100 are not permitted
- Each debater receives an individual score and a rank from 1 (best) to 8 (worst)
- Ranks must be distinct — no ties
- No "low-point wins" — speaker points don't determine team ranking, but teams ranked higher should generally have better speaker points as a sanity check
- Choose the LOWEST category a speech falls into (limited by weakest criterion)

## Scale Criteria

| Score Range | Warrant Quality | Impact Quality | Weighing Quality | Engagement | Argument Quality |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **50-54** | No warrant or completely irrelevant reasoning | No discernible impact or entirely unwarranted | Not done at all | Ignored virtually all opposing arguments | Speech was actively harmful to their side |
| **55-59** | Warrant is barely present, largely assertion | Impact is vague, unexplained, or irrelevant to the debate | Not done or merely asserted | Missed nearly all key responses, critically impaired team | Very poor, numerous major errors |
| **60-64** | Warrant present but weak, easily refuted | Impact stated but magnitude or mechanism unclear | Attempted but poorly justified | Several key responses missed, significantly impaired team | Below average, significant weaknesses throughout |
| **65-69** | Warrant adequate but not compelling | Impact present but not well-connected to warrant | Done partially, insufficient justification | Some key responses missed, moderate impairment | Average but below par, competent but unremarkable |
| **70-74** | Solid warrant, clear but not decisive | Clear impact with reasonable magnitude | Weighing done but could be more comparative | Most key responses made, some gaps | Solid average speech, competent across the board |
| **75-79** | Strong warrant, gives clear reason to believe | Well-connected impact with clear magnitude and scope | Weighing is comparative and well-justified | All key responses made, strong engagement | Above average, clearly above the median |
| **80-84** | Nearly decisive warrant, hard to refute | Impact is clearly explained with precise scope and magnitude | Weighing is thorough, comparative, and decisive | Excellent engagement, no important drops | Excellent speech, strong all-around performance |
| **85-89** | Decisive warrant, establishes near-certainty | Impact is precise, well-scoped, and central to the debate | Weighing is near-perfect, decisive and well-justified | Flawless engagement, anticipates and pre-empts | Outstanding, near-perfect execution |
| **90-94** | Decisive in context of opposing warrants | Impact perfectly tailored, RFD-determinative | Weighing is debate-determinative, fully developed | No possible improvement in engagement | Exceptional, debate-changing performance |
| **95-100** | Decisive and contextually superior to all opposing reasoning | Impact perfectly scoped, magnitude and kind precisely calibrated | Weighing is flawless, fully justifies the decision | Perfect engagement plus strategic value beyond responses | Near-perfect, one of the best speeches in the tournament |

## Special Notes for BP

### Whip Speeches (GW, OW)
- Whip speeches are NOT constructive speeches — they crystallize, weigh, and summarize
- Whips should NOT introduce new arguments; any new argumentation in a whip should be penalized
- Score whips on: strategic voting, crystallization quality, weighing, and comparison — NOT on new material

### Extension Speeches (MG, MO)
- MG and MO are extension speeches for closing teams
- These speeches should bring NEW, DISTINCTIVE material that differentiates CG/CO from OG/OO
- Reward genuine extension (new framing, new impact calculus, new argument layer)
- Penalize mere repetition of opening team's material without differentiation

### Opening Speeches (PM, DPM, LO, DLO)
- Opening speeches set up the debate framework
- Score on: case construction, warrant quality, impact framing, and strategic setup for closing teammates
- Opening teams that leave no room for their closing teammates to extend may be penalized in relative comparison

### Practical Calibration
- 75 is a solid, truly average speech. A competent debater who does everything right but nothing exceptional gets a 75.
- 70-74 is below average — the debater was competent but had noticeable gaps or weaknesses.
- 65-69 is noticeably below average — significant errors or omissions.
- 80-84 is excellent — strong engagement, clear argumentation, good weighing.
- 85-89 is outstanding — near-perfect execution, debate-changing moments.
- 90+ is exceptional — rare, reserved for speeches that fundamentally shift the debate.
